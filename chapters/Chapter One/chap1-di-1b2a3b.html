A couple more minutes later, an impressive-looking vehicle pulled to a stop in front of you. You frowned when you noticed that the 'bus' had no wheels and was therefore hovering over the ground by an inch or so. What's more, the vehicle appeared almost of translucent quality, like something that couldn't be physically touched.

Perhaps it was a hologram, or some light-based concept, as many things in this universe seemed to rely on.

But the door opened for you nonetheless, so you boarded. That's when you realized you had no idea how to pay your fare, so you awkwardly glanced at the AT's piloter. Cringing internally, you mustered up the courage to ask a seemingly stupid question.

"How does one pay for the fare again?"

Your piloter was unfortunately not a very amiable person, which he demonstrated by shriveling up his nose as he glanced at you.

"You sure are flaunting your social status, aren't you? You're doing a great job of announcing that you've never taken an AT before. That your sterisia you're holding?"

You nodded, glancing at the device in your hand, secretly glad that you had looked up its name earlier in your room, though you hadn't thought as far as to research the methods of payment. Probably due to the consistent feeling of familiarity yet foreignness you'd been encountering throughout your time in this strange universe. Some things were obvious, and some things were not.

What misfortune.

The piloter glanced at his dashboard and waved you inside.

"Your sterisia has auto-fare-pay set up. My records say you paid on entry. You may now go take a seat, young lady."

You exhaled a sigh of relief as you heeded his advice and took a seat far in the back, away from your unpleasant piloter. Luckily, the AT wasn't so crowded, so you had plenty of leg room and seat options to choose from.

As you glanced out the window to see the passing blur of your neighborhood, you shuddered. What would have happened if it turned out you had no way of paying? Of course, you weren't left without options, since you could have also always just sought out your personal piloter and tell him you changed your mind, but since you were already on the AT, it would have been cumbersome to have to change plans midway.

Noticing a window handle, you experimented a bit, trying to figure out how to open the window a sliver. As you had expected, though the handle was solid, it felt subtly warm, like your hand just barely happened to not phase right through it. With minimal effort, you pulled it open to enjoy the breeze. It was raining out, like how your personal piloter had mentioned it might, but you didn't mind.

You vaguely remarked that the AT pulled to a stop, letting on more passengers. You glanced briefly at the doors, before facing out your window again. After all, you wouldn't have expected the new passengers to sit right beside you on a near-empty AT, let alone address you.

"Oh wow~! If it isn't Miss Polaris."

You looked to your side to meet the eyes of the two young men that sat beside you.

... What?

How could a person look so... let alone two of them in a row... You quickly pushed the thought aside, remarking at once that you recognized these two -obviously- twins from one of the framed photos on Polaris' desk. You had originally thought that the photo was too generous, but now you realized that the photo hadn't done justice at all.

The one that was seated right beside you exuded unadulterated confidence, a teasing grin pulling the left corner of his lips just slightly as he looked at you with a frustratingly charming pair of eyes. In a brief moment, his gaze drifted towards the top of your head in a subconscious sort of manner, though they returned to yours soon after.

"You seem awfully normal for someone that was dead to the world until recently. Everything okay?"

You were about to reassure him, like you had been doing to every new person you met, when his smirk took on a somewhat vexatious quality.

"That pretty head of yours sure is unlucky. After all, it's stuck to your shoulders."

"... Sorry, what?"

This man did not.

He struggled to stifle a laugh at your reaction, before composing himself remarkably well.

"No but seriously, is your head okay? You took quite the hit."

"Perfectly fine, in fact, thank you."

He nodded, his expression turning mockingly contemplative.

"I can see that. What I can't is believe how you actually managed to get a concussion. You really didn't see it coming?"

You didn't know the exact semantics behind your concussion, so you didn't really know how to refute his jab. After all, in the end, the one who got the concussion was Polaris and not you, so there was a chance Polaris was a ditz.

"..."

He observed your expression when you refused to retort, his confident smile diminishing just barely, as if deep in thought.

"And to think, when you stopped contacting me, I thought I had finally gotten rid of you."

So much for thinking that the change in expression meant he would stop.

His twin tossed a stern glare at his brother, before leaning forward to give you a bubbly smile that somewhat soothed your new-found irritation.

"Sorry, Polaris. You know how Castor is. Are you feeling better? We were really worried for you when we heard the news. Castor included."

The twin gave a pointed look at his brother as he accented his name, raising an eyebrow subtly as if daring him to refute.

Castor. So that's the pompous jerk's name. Talk about irritating, in all sense of the word. After all, you could and would have disliked him in peace, if it weren't for his omnipotent face card.

"Thanks for asking... I'm feeling alright now."

You returned the smile, your mind already made up on who the better twin was.

Castor initially seemed unfazed by his brother's remark, though he did sit up a bit in response to the unspoken scolding. He studied you intently for a moment, tilting his head swiftly to blow the bangs out of his eyes as he spoke up again.

"You sure you didn't forget anything? You know memory loss, both temporary and permanent, can result from concussions. I honestly wouldn't be surprised, since yours was kinda serious. Quick, what's two plus three?"

You honestly felt insulted as you stared back at the impish glint in his eyes. How would you not know that?

"Five. I don't think memory loss works like that."

His smile turned a bit more genuine as he let slip a nice chuckle for once, seemingly more at ease at the fact you were willing to snap back.

"Mm. Good. Seems like you've still got the attitude. Seriously though, are you even allowed to be out already? You're so careless sometimes, that I wouldn't be surprised if I found you in a ditch."

"I think I would know better than to be stubborn about my own well-being."

"Sure. Let's leave it at that."

He glanced at you one last time with a satisfied expression, before turning to his brother, his attitude somewhat confusingly different as his seemingly-omnipresent smirk smoothed into one of gentle consideration.

"Pollux."

His twin, whom you now knew was Pollux, looked up from his sterisia and tilted his head curiously.

"You done grilling her?"

"Sorry."

Ain't no way Castor knew how to apologize.

Castor leaned away from you to look over at Pollux's phone, and the two of them discussed something you assumed was gift shopping, with either of them occasionally saying things like, 'she doesn't like scarves', or 'that's an ugly colour'.

Just when you thought they were going to leave you in silence for the rest of the ride, Pollux gave you a warm smile.

"Polaris, where are you headed?"

"I'm going to uh... Lyx Station. To help out a friend with something."

"You too? We're headed to Aquila Business District to go shopping for our mother's birthday."

Pollux grinned.

"Are you volunteering with Xihe again?"

He laughed pleasantly.

"She's always doing the oddest things. You must never have a boring day in your life with her as a friend."

Castor scoffed and gave you an irritating smile.

"Why would Xihe even want you to help her out anyway? You're about as useful as a rock."

"Am not. How would you know?"

"If you say so."

He shrugged nonchalantly as he turned away from you to Pollux, struggling to prevent the twitch at the corner of his mouth at your reaction while he scrolled through his sterisia, occasionally showing some possible gift options to his brother. He might as well have smiled, since his eyes said it all.

The rest of the AT ride passed by in a similar fashion, and you earned the chance to get to know the twins better. You definitely liked Pollux better. But... if you really thought about it, Castor wasn't so bad either. His insults were all just worries and concerns in disguise. Though you still despised that perfect smile of his.

"The next stop is, Lyx, Lyx Station."

The three of you got ready to leave, and soon enough, the AT pulled to a final stop. You looked up somewhat startled as Castor took ahold of your shoulder with surprising gentleness while passing by.

"Don't get yourself killed. I know that's one of your only talents, but next week, I better be seeing you in class and not in a coffin. See you."

And he was off.
